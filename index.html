Bing

This project, developed by the 2nd year of the Informatics technical course at link Instituto Federal Farroupilha - Santo Augusto Campus, aims to create an informational website about the Bing translation tool. Under the development of students Breno Lucas Cavalheiro, Gabriel Cavalheiro da Silva, and Poliana Vitoria Boehm, and with the teachers coordination of Alexandra Buzanelo Schossler, Cleiton Jose Richter, and Tamara Angelica B. da Rosa, the website aims to provide a conceptual overview of the tool, addressing its features and practical applications. Integrating knowledge from the disciplines of Programming, English Language, and Geography.

This project seeks to create an informative website about the translation tool Bing, developed by Microsoft, whose main function is to facilitate access to information available on the web. It allows users to search for a wide variety of topics, offering results that include websites, images, videos, news, and maps, in addition to integrating with other Microsoft services such as Office and Edge. Headquartered in Redmond, Washington, Bing also stands out for its artificial intelligence tools, such as search suggestions and instant answers, which enhance the user experience by providing relevant information quickly and efficiently.


Bing, developed by Microsoft, was launched in 2009 as an evolution of the former Live Search, under the leadership of Satya Nadella, who at the time was Vice President of the Online Services Division. Microsoft created Bing with the aim of directly competing with Google, offering a simplified interface and innovative features such as "Visual Search," which allowed users to refine search results in a more intuitive and efficient way. Since its launch, Bing has sought to differentiate itself through exclusive features and deeper integration with other Microsoft products such as the Internet Explorer browser and the Windows operating system.

Over the years, Bing has incorporated advanced artificial intelligence technologies and developed features such as image search, voice search, and integration with the virtual assistant Cortana. These innovations have helped Microsoft position Bing as a solid alternative to Google, especially in markets such as the United States, where it has achieved significant market share. Although Google continues to dominate the search engine market, Bing has remained relevant and is frequently used on Microsoft devices and services, reinforcing the company's presence in the online search sector.

The Bing search engine offers various advanced features, such as image search, where users can upload an image to find visually similar content, and video search, which allows users to preview videos directly on the search results page. Voice search facilitates quick queries using spoken commands, while entity data integration provides contextual answers about people, places, and events. Additionally, Bing Maps offers detailed navigation with 3D views and real-time traffic information, and Bing News and Bing Shopping aggregate news from reliable sources and compare product prices across different online stores.
